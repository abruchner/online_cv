My Background.

Civil Engineering

I studied civil engineering at the University of Applied Sciences in Nuremberg and graduated in 2004 as civil engineer. One year later this type of study had changed and was offered as either a Bachelor or a Master study. So I am one of the last 'Dipl.Ing.(FH)'. My strategic focus was on construction operation, which emphasized the topics of calculations, resource planning, project management and building law.

Facility Management

Two years after finishing my first study, I did extra-occupational my Masters degree in Facility Management. This covered all FM related topics like technical FM, operational FM, infrastructural FM, CAFM, quality management, benchmarking, space management, etc.

Business Manager

Finally, I did another study, called Business Management for Engineers, which covered similar topics as my other two studies, but also included industrial accounting, investments and financing, logistics, labor legislation, contract law, competition law and marketing.

My Carreer Path.

SERENDIPITYREMIX, since 2018

I joined Serendipityremix in 2018 as Senior Facility Management Specialist. My projects are very versatile and I learned a lot during my time there. Our biggest project was for a church group. For approximately 1.5 years we did building condition assessments for about 200 churches all over South Africa, including short-, mid- and long term maintenance budgets and proposed a FM strategy. For six months I also joined another project to develop a Facility Management Strategy for the City of Tshwane / Pretoria. I also had a hospital project for a big hospital in Durban, for which an assessment of the structure, building fabric and costs for the whole lifecycle were required.

NERA GROUP, 2017

At Nera, which was a start-up, I was employed as Director Facilities. My job was doing FM hospital assessments, which included the building structures, as well as the FM services. The hospitals were middle-class hospitals in South Africa, Kenya, Egypt, Tunesia and Morocco. We provided FM reports containing the assessments and recommendations for FM services and maintenance. These included for example proposing outsourcing platforms or bundling of activities, change of contracts, purchasing more energy efficient equipment, etc. We also targeted Health and Safety concerns.

SIEMENS (PTY) LTD, 2013-2016

I joined Siemens South Africa as a delegate in 2013 and was employed as Head of Assetmanagement Africa, responsible for all Real Estate assets that Siemens had on the African continent. The properties were mainly in South Africa, Nigeria, Morocco and Tunisia, with some additional smaller offices in other countries like Ghana or Mozambique. The portfolio consisted of production/assembly facilities and offices. Our main focus was on reducing owned space by selling assets (sometimes with lease-back optione) or consolidating facilities. In the Head Office we refurbished the entrance area, the canteen, implemented a kindergarden and reduced the energy consumption for cooling systems with the installation of ice containers on the roofs.

SIEMENS AG, 2012

After participating in the European Junior Talent Circle, an internal development program for Siemens employees (under 30) with management experience, I was promoted to Head of Location Management Erlangen and my responsibilities moved towards office buildings. I did not stay very long in this position, as the opportunity of going abroad knocked on the door nearly at the same time. I had always wanted to work and live in another country and to meet and work with people from other cultures and with different backgrounds.

SIEMENS AG, 2004-2012

I joined the production facility of Automation and Drives already as working student doing AutoCad plans. Over the time I took over more and more responsibilities and was taken over as Team Member Facility Managementafter I had finished my studies. The location was about 200,000 sqm in size and had twelve production halls, in which large drives and control cabinets in various sizes were manufactured and tested for ships, trains, paper mills, mining trucks, etc. In 2007 I took over the Head of Facility Management position and in 2010 I was moved to Siemens Real Estate due to a reorganization in the company. I was then Location Manager, still at the same site, but more in the role of 'owner' of the location and dealing with my previous working colleagues as 'tenants'. My area of responsibility on the site changed over the years. While in the first years I was very much involved in project management (planning, tendering, calculations) of changes to buildings, maintenance topics or machine foundations / production relocations, from 2010 onwards I took on more managerial tasks including people management, location strategies and especially financial topics like forcasting and budgeting.
